# Hybrid Dropshipping Model (HDM) — Transition Playbook

The single biggest margin upgrade in Indian dropshipping is **moving from third-party supplier to direct private supplier on validated winners**. Dropdash / Roposo are perfect for *finding* winners (no inventory risk, fast launches) but they leave 30-45% margin on the table once a SKU proves itself. This skill is when, why, and how to make the switch.

**Default rule:** keep new SKUs on Dropdash until they hit the HDM trigger. Don't pre-emptively go private — you'll eat dead stock on a SKU that flops.

---

## When to transition — HDM trigger conditions

A SKU is eligible for HDM transition only when **ALL** of these are true for 7+ consecutive days:

| # | Trigger | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| T1 | Confirmed orders per day (after COD verification) | ≥ 10/day sustained 7 days |
| T2 | CPP stable at or under Target CPP | ≤ 8% of SP, no upward drift |
| T3 | Hook Rate on the lead creative | ≥ 30% sustained (creative is not the bottleneck) |
| T4 | RTO rate on current supplier | < 30% (otherwise fix RTO first, not supplier) |
| T5 | At least 2 ad sets profitable, not just 1 | Reduces single-creative dependence risk |
| T6 | Inventory turnover proves real demand | ≥ 60 units shipped in the 7-day window |

Below 10 confirmed orders/day = stay on Dropdash. The 20-piece private supplier MOQ + ₹3-5k upfront capital makes earlier transition unprofitable.

## Why HDM works — the margin math

A typical SKU on Dropdash / Roposo:
- Supplier cost (Dropdash): ₹350
- Sell price: ₹999
- Gross margin: ₹649 (65%)
- After ads (CPP ₹80) + RTO (30% × ₹200): ₹509 (51% net)

The same SKU on HDM with a Tier-1 IndiaMART supplier:
- Direct supplier cost: ₹180-220 (40-50% cheaper)
- Sell price: ₹999 (unchanged)
- Gross margin: ₹779-819 (78-82%)
- After ads + RTO: ₹639-679 (64-68% net)
- **+13-17 percentage points of net margin per order** = effectively a 25-33% increase in net profit

This is why scaled operators *always* migrate winners.

## The 20-piece test batch protocol (course-tested)

Before you commit to a private supplier, run a structured 20-piece test. Coach Deep's EEA method:

### Step 1 — Source 3 supplier candidates (IndiaMART, JustDial, local market)
- Search IndiaMART for the exact product (use product type + 2 key spec terms)
- Filter: location ≤ 600km of your fulfillment hub (cuts forward shipping ₹15-25)
- Filter: trust signal — TrustSEAL or Verified Member, ≥3 yrs on platform, photo product gallery
- Negotiate three quotes hard. Floor price is ~45-55% below Dropdash for the same SKU
- **Never accept the first quote.** "Final price kya hai?" is the magic phrase — it almost always unlocks 8-15% more

### Step 2 — Sample order from top 2 (₹500-1,500 each)
- Order 1-2 units to your home address
- Time the delivery (must arrive ≤ 5 days for Tier-2 city, ≤ 3 for metro)
- Inspect: packaging quality, defect rate, product feel, scent (synthetic ≠ premium)
- **Photograph unboxing for creatives** — use this to A/B test against current Dropdash images

### Step 3 — 20-piece pilot batch (validate at small scale)
- Buy 20 units from the winning sample supplier
- Run them through your existing fulfillment with **mandatory phone-call verification** on every order (overrides verification tiers for this batch only)
- Track: defect rate, RTO rate, NDR rate, customer feedback, supplier responsiveness on replacements
- **Pass criteria** (must hit all):
  - Defect rate < 5% (1 or 0 defects in 20)
  - RTO rate < 25% (5 or fewer in 20)
  - Supplier responds to replacement request in ≤ 24h
  - No quality drift between sample and bulk order
- **Fail criteria** (any one):
  - Defect rate ≥ 10% → try the #2 supplier with another 20-piece test
  - RTO rate ≥ 35% → root-cause first (was it product, targeting, or creative?) per `rto-prevention` Layer 6
  - Supplier ghosts on replacement → instant DQ, never use again

### Step 4 — Scale to 100-piece MOQ once pilot passes
- Now you're ready to commit to a 100-piece MOQ at the negotiated price
- Coordinate with `inventory-planner` for storage / direct-to-3PL flow
- Hold a 21-day inventory buffer at projected post-transition velocity (private suppliers don't ship same-day like Dropdash)
- Keep the Dropdash listing live as a fallback for 14 days post-transition (in case private supplier has a hiccup)

## Supplier vetting checklist (IndiaMART / JustDial)

Score each candidate 0-10 — only proceed with suppliers scoring ≥ 7/10:

| # | Signal | YES = 1pt | NO = 0pt |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | TrustSEAL or Verified Member on IndiaMART | ✓ | ✗ |
| 2 | ≥3 years on the platform | ✓ | ✗ |
| 3 | ≥10 product photos (not just stock images) | ✓ | ✗ |
| 4 | Located ≤ 600km of your primary fulfillment | ✓ | ✗ |
| 5 | Phone number works, supplier picks up within 2 rings | ✓ | ✗ |
| 6 | Speaks reasonable English OR Hindi (not just regional) | ✓ | ✗ |
| 7 | Offers GST invoice (essential for input credit) | ✓ | ✗ |
| 8 | MOQ ≤ 100 pieces (not 500+) | ✓ | ✗ |
| 9 | Offers replacement on defect (not just refund) | ✓ | ✗ |
| 10 | Has WhatsApp Business (not just personal WhatsApp) | ✓ | ✗ |

Below 7/10 = pass. Indian supplier base is huge; you can always find a better one.

## Dual-supplier strategy (don't single-source)

Once HDM transition completes, keep a **second active supplier** running at 20% of volume:
- Primary supplier: 80% of orders (best price + relationship)
- Backup supplier: 20% of orders (insurance against primary's hiccups)
- Rotate backup quarterly to keep relationships warm
- During Diwali / festival peak: ramp backup to 40-50% (primary will be overloaded)

A single-source private supplier WILL hiccup at the worst time (festival rush, family emergency, factory shutdown). Two suppliers = sleep at night.

---

## When NOT to transition (stay on Dropdash)

Keep the SKU on third-party even if it hits some triggers, if:

- **Product is seasonal with < 60 days remaining** in the window — you'll be stuck with dead stock
- **Margin is already 60%+ on Dropdash** — the marginal upside from HDM doesn't justify 20-piece capital tie-up
- **Operator cash position < 30-day buffer** — HDM transition requires ₹15-50k upfront for first MOQ; protect runway first
- **RTO root cause is product quality complaints** — Dropdash will discontinue, HDM exposes you to the same quality risk just with capital tied up
- **Trademark / IP risk on the SKU** — private sourcing makes YOU the liable party; let Dropdash carry that risk

## RTO reduction from HDM (expected delta)

Private suppliers typically reduce RTO by 5-15 percentage points because:
1. **Their own COD verification calls** — many do this before dispatch as part of their service
2. **Better packaging** — branded boxes signal legitimacy to the buyer at the door
3. **Faster delivery** — shorter forward leg = less buyer time-to-regret
4. **Quality consistency** — fewer "not what I expected" rejections at the door

Expected post-HDM RTO: typically 15-22% (down from 25-35% on third-party).

## Handoff

- HDM trigger conditions met → `inventory-planner` flags for operator approval
- Operator approves → `product-research` runs supplier vetting (IndiaMART + JustDial searches)
- Sample order arrives → operator inspects, `creative-studio` re-shoots unboxing for new UGC
- 20-piece pilot passes → `inventory-planner` coordinates 100-piece MOQ + 3PL routing
- 100-piece batch live → `customer-support` updates COD verification scripts for new supplier
- Post-transition RTO measured at Day 30 → `performance-coach` includes in weekly health score

## Hard rules

- **Never** transition a SKU below 10 confirmed orders/day for 7 days. The 20-piece capital + supplier-relationship overhead doesn't pay back below this threshold.
- **Never** skip the 20-piece pilot. Going straight to 100-piece MOQ on an un-vetted supplier = you'll eat ₹15-40k of dead stock when defects show up.
- **Never** single-source post-transition. Always run a second supplier at 20% backup volume.
- **Never** abandon the Dropdash listing on Day 1 of HDM. Keep it live as fallback for 14 days while you stabilize.
- **Never** transition a seasonal product within 60 days of the window closing.
- **Never** transition a product with quality complaints in `customer-support` logs — fix the product or kill the SKU first.
- **Always** demand GST invoice from the private supplier. Without it, you lose 18% input credit = the HDM margin advantage evaporates.
